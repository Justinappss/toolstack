## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is an accumulator bet?**

An accumulator (or acca) is a single bet that combines two or more individual selections. All selections must win for the bet to pay out. The odds multiply together, so accumulators offer much larger potential returns than single bets \u2014 but the risk increases with every leg you add.

**Q: What is the difference between decimal, fractional and American odds?**

Decimal odds (e.g. 2.50) show the total return per \u00a31 staked including your stake back. Fractional odds (e.g. 6/4) show profit relative to stake \u2014 6/4 means \u00a36 profit for every \u00a34 staked. American odds use +/- notation: +250 means \u00a3250 profit on a \u00a3100 stake, while -150 means you must stake \u00a3150 to win \u00a3100 profit.

**Q: How do you calculate accumulator returns?**

Convert all odds to decimal format, then multiply them together to get the combined decimal odds. Multiply the combined odds by your stake to get the total return. Subtract your stake from the total return to get profit. Example: 2.0 \u00d7 3.0 \u00d7 1.5 = 9.0 combined odds. \u00a310 stake \u00d7 9.0 = \u00a390 return, \u00a380 profit.

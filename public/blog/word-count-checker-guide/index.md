## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does word count directly affect Google rankings?**

Word count alone does not directly determine rankings — Google does not have a minimum word count requirement. What matters is whether your content fully covers the topic. That said, most pages that rank on page one for competitive keywords tend to be comprehensive (1,000–2,500 words) because they answer more of what a searcher needs. Thin content (under 300 words) can be flagged by Google's quality systems if it doesn't fully satisfy the search intent.

---

**Q: What is a good Flesch readability score?**

Flesch Reading Ease scores run from 0 (very difficult) to 100 (very easy). For general web content, aim for 60–70 (plain English, easily understood by most adults). Blog posts and marketing copy typically score best at 65–75. Academic or technical writing may score 30–50. Scores below 30 are hard to read for most audiences and typically see higher bounce rates.

---

**Q: How do I improve my readability score?**

The two biggest levers are sentence length and word complexity. Shorten sentences to an average of 15–20 words — split any sentence over 30 words. Replace long words with short ones: "utilise" → "use", "implement" → "build", "approximately" → "about". These changes alone typically move a score by 10–15 points.

---

**Q: Does readability affect AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude)?**

Yes. AI systems that summarise or cite content from the web (Perplexity, Bing Copilot, ChatGPT with web access) prefer clearly structured, readable content that's easy to extract key facts from. Content with short paragraphs, clear headings, and direct answers is cited more frequently than dense prose. Improving readability also improves AI discoverability.

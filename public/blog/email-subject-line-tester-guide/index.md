**Q: What subject line length gets the best open rates?**

Mobile inboxes show 30–40 characters before truncating. Desktop Gmail shows roughly 60 characters. The optimal range for maximum open rates across devices is 28–50 characters — long enough to communicate value, short enough to display fully on mobile. Subject lines over 70 characters consistently underperform on mobile-heavy lists.

---

**Q: What are power words in subject lines?**

Power words are emotionally charged terms that trigger curiosity, urgency, exclusivity, or desire. Examples: "free", "instantly", "exclusive", "limited", "secret", "proven", "guaranteed". Used sparingly, they increase open rates. Overused or combined with spam triggers, they send your email to the promotions tab or junk folder.

---

**Q: Does subject line personalisation actually improve open rates?**

Yes — subject lines with the recipient's first name improve open rates by 20-26% on average (Experian). But only if your list data is clean. A personalisation tag that pulls blank or incorrect data ("[FNAME], this is for you") destroys trust instantly. Test personalisation on a segment before rolling it out to your full list.

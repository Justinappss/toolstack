## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why does Google rewrite my meta descriptions?**

Google rewrites meta descriptions when it judges your description doesn't accurately represent the page content for a specific search query. It pulls alternative text from your page body that it thinks better matches what the searcher is looking for. Writing query-aligned, content-matching descriptions reduces rewrite rates significantly.

---

**Q: What is the ideal meta description length?**

Between 150 and 160 characters. Google truncates longer descriptions with an ellipsis in the SERP, typically around 160 characters on desktop and 130 characters on mobile. Shorter descriptions (under 120 characters) often get rewritten because Google sees them as incomplete. The ToolStack meta description generator shows a real-time character count and live SERP preview.

---

**Q: Do meta descriptions affect Google rankings?**

Meta descriptions are not a direct ranking factor — Google confirmed this. However, they indirectly affect rankings by influencing click-through rate (CTR). A compelling description that accurately reflects the page content and matches searcher intent drives higher CTR, which sends positive engagement signals. Write descriptions for the human reader, not for the algorithm.

---

**Q: What should every meta description include?**

A strong meta description includes: the primary keyword (ideally near the start), a clear value proposition (what the reader gets), a soft call to action (e.g. "Learn how", "See the full guide"), and a character count under 160. Avoid keyword stuffing — Google ignores it and readers tune it out.

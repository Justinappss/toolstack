Google AI Overviews now appear on over 40% of all searches — and the sites being cited inside them are not necessarily the ones ranked number one. Traditional SEO gets you into the blue links. GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) gets you inside the AI answer itself, which sits above everything else on the page.

## What Is GEO and Why Does It Matter in 2026?

GEO — Generative Engine Optimisation — is the discipline of structuring your content to be cited inside AI-generated answers. Every time Google's AI answers a question, it pulls from existing web pages and credits sources with a small attribution chip. AI citation selection criteria differ from traditional SEO ranking signals: answer clarity, content structure, topical depth, and schema markup determine citation far more than backlinks alone.

## How Google AI Overviews Selects Its Sources

Pages regularly cited in AI Overviews share four characteristics: the answer appears in the first paragraph, headers are phrased as questions, the page contains specific verifiable facts, and FAQPage schema is present.

## The 5-Step GEO Framework

**Step 1 — Lead With the Answer:** The first paragraph after your H1 must directly state the answer to your target query. Complete, standalone, accurate in one or two sentences.

**Step 2 — Rewrite H2s as Real Questions:** Convert heading labels to the actual questions your audience searches. Use Google's People Also Ask for exact phrasing.

**Step 3 — Add a Proper FAQ Section:** Six to eight questions from People Also Ask, answered in under 80 words each, with FAQPage JSON-LD schema applied.

**Step 4 — Build Topical Clusters:** One main guide + three to five supporting posts + internal links between them. Topical depth signals authority to Google's AI.

**Step 5 — Add Structured Data Markup:** ArticleSchema, FAQPage schema, and BreadcrumbList are the three most important. Pages without schema require more AI interpretation — and get cited less.

## Traditional SEO vs GEO

| Factor | Traditional SEO | GEO (2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Goal | Rank #1 in blue links | Get cited inside AI answer |
| Content style | Keyword density | Direct Q&A format |
| Headers | Topic labels | Real questions |
| Page structure | Long introductions | Answer first |
| Schema | Optional | Required baseline |
| Metric | Click-through rate | AI citation rate |

## Quick Wins You Can Do Today

1. Add a direct answer in the first paragraph of your top three pages
2. Rewrite two or three H2s as questions from People Also Ask
3. Add five FAQ entries under 80 words each
4. Add FAQPage JSON-LD schema to those FAQ entries

These four changes per page take roughly 45 minutes and can produce AI Overview citations within 2–6 weeks.

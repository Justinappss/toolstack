## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How do I extract keywords from a job description?**

Copy the full job description into a Google Doc. Use Ctrl+F to find the verbs and nouns that repeat 3+ times — those are the exact signals the hiring manager (and ATS) is looking for. Aim to pull 5–8 priority keywords. Common examples: "cross-functional collaboration", "data-driven", "stakeholder management", "growth targets". Focus on action verbs and outcomes, not just job titles.

---

**Q: Does tailoring a cover letter really make a difference?**

Yes — significantly. Generic cover letters are detected immediately and often ignored. A letter that mirrors the job description's language, references the company by name, and addresses specific requirements shows the hiring manager you actually read the posting. ATS systems also score letters for keyword density before they reach a human reader.

---

**Q: How long should a tailored cover letter be?**

Three to four short paragraphs — one page maximum. Opening paragraph: why this role and this company specifically. Second paragraph: your most relevant achievement with a number if possible. Third paragraph: one skill that directly maps to a key requirement. Closing: clear CTA (looking forward to discussing). Longer letters are rarely read in full.

---

**Q: Can I use the same cover letter for multiple jobs?**

A completely generic letter hurts more than it helps. However, you can create a strong base letter and personalise 30–40% for each application — the company name, the specific role, one tailored achievement, and a reference to something unique about the company.

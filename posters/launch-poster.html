what works
01 · THE FRICTION TAX

The internet got greedy with simple things.

Every utility site now asks for an email, runs a 30-second ad, or wants a credit card before it'll format a JSON file. We accepted terrible UX as the norm. This issue is the reset.

"5 sites asked me to sign up before they'd format JSON. So I closed my laptop and started building."
02 · BUILT IN PUBLIC

57 tools, 90 days, one solo founder.

AI Prompt Generator. JSON Formatter. Mortgage Calculator. Pack Break EV. World Cup Accumulator. Each one shipped in 2–4 hours once the layout, SEO and structure were templated.

"Templating the boring 80% turns 57 tools into a 90-day project — not a 90-month one."
03 · NO SIGN-UP IS THE MARKETING

When the wall comes down, the URL travels.

People share what they don't have to pay for with their email. "No signup" became the differentiator no competitor was using — and the entire distribution loop follows from it.

"Friction kills referral. Make the tool work in one click and the link travels itself."
04 · DISTRIBUTION > PRODUCT

One Reddit post beat three weeks of SEO.

ToolStack hit #1 on r/SideProject with 6,100 views in 24 hours. The lesson: pick the room before the script. The first hour of engagement decides the next 60 days of reach.

"Engagement velocity in the first 60 minutes is the only metric the algorithm actually rewards."
05 · ADSENSE PAYS THE RENT

You don't need Stripe to validate an idea.

Light banner ads cover hosting, domain, and a small monthly profit. The cheap channel keeps the product free, which keeps it shareable, which keeps the channel cheap. The loop pays itself.

"AdSense is the rent. The tools are the home. Don't confuse the two."
06 · WHAT'S NEXT

Niche calculators outperform generic ones.

Coming up: more World Cup 2026 utilities, deeper card-flip math, a Chrome extension once we hit 5K weekly users. Build for the audience already searching — they're easier to find than the one you're inventing.

"Don't build a market. Build a tool for the market that already exists in someone's open tabs."
PRO TIP The simplest moat is being the only tool in the category that doesn't try to take something from the user.
